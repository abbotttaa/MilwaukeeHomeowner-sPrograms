Compliance Loan Program

City of Milwaukee

The Program

The Compliance Loan Program, administered by the Department of Neighborhood Services, provides eligible homeowners with a 0% interest, deferred payment loan to correct any oustanding code violations. The loans are secured by a mortgage which will be paid when the property is transfered or sold. CLP is a full-service program and provides approved contractors as well as project oversight with a licensed inspector.

Additional Eligibility/Participation Requirements

  • The total cost of repairs cannot exceed $15,000
  • Owner cannot be in any chapter of bankruptcy other than Chapter 13
  • Total Household Income must be no greater than 50% County Median Income(CMI)

Area of Availability

Citywide

STRONG Homes Loan

City of Milwaukee

The Program

Through the Neighborhood Improvement Development Corporation, the STRONG Homes Loan Program offers partially forgivable loans of up to $20,000 throughout the city of Milwaukee.* Loans can be used to make emergency and essential home repairs and address building code orders. Loans are repayable, with a deferred payment option available for homeowners who are low income and elderly or disabled. Technical assistance will be provided to homeowners under that program to assist with rehabilitation projects.

* Forgivable loans may be limited if applicant has recieved previous forgivable funds from the City of Milwaukee

Additional Eligibility/Participation Requirements

  • Household income qualifications (see income guidelines at the website provided below)

Targeted Investment Neighborhoods (TINS)

City of Milwaukee

The Program

The program provides no interest and forgivable loans for property improvements in targeted geographical areas. Assistance is available to both homeowners and responsible rental property owners. Technical assistance is also provided to help create a scope of work, obtain bids for the work and manage the construction process.

Additional Eligibility/Participation Requirements for Homeowners

  • Household Income must be less than 80% of Area Median Income (see chart at website provided below)
  • Flexible underwriting guidelines, but must have a history of paying bills on time

Additional Eligibility/Participation Requirements for Rental Property Owners

  • Must have a responsible track record of owning and managing rental property in the city
  • Must have attended City of Milwaukee Landlord Training Class
  • Rental Property owners must match the amout of assistance they are receiving on a dollar for dollar basis and show proof of matching funds
  • Units must generally have two or more bedrooms
  • Units must be rented to, and affordable to families earning under 60% of Area Median Income for five years after closinng (see chart at website provided below)

Area of Availability

The program is available in nine targeted investment neighborhoods (see website provided below)

Neighborhood Improvement Project (NIP)

City of Milwaukee

The Program

The program assists eligible owner occupants in repairing their homes. The minimum scope of work to qualify for the program is $5,000, with a maximum of $24,500 per home. Eligible work includes code violations, maintenance and mechanical work. Assistance is provided through local NIP service agencies (listed at the website provided below) working with local contractors.

Additional Eligibility/Participation Requirements

  • Household Income must be at or below 60% of Area Median Income (see website provided below)
  • Must not have recieved prior NIP or other City home repair assistance

Area of Availability

Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) Area (see map at website provided below)

Me2 Milwaukee Energy Efficiency Program

City of Milwaukee

The Program

The program provides low interest loans of up to $15,000 in partnership with Summit Credit Union to make energy efficient improvements to your home. Eligible improvements include attic and wall insulation, air sealing (caulking and sealing gaps that let in outside air), ENERGY STARÂ® rated windows, Focus on Energy eligible heating and cooling equipment, hot water heaters, heat pumps and supporting improvements (up to 20% of total loan, e.g. minor electrical upgrades).

Additional Eligibility/Participation Requirements

  • Owner of building must be listed as an individual and not as a trust, LLC or form of business
  • No delinquent property taxes due to the City of Milwaukee
  • Work must be performed by a participating Me2 contractor
  • No maximum income limits

To Qualify for a Me2 loan through Summit Credit Union, the applicant must

  • Be employed for the past 12-24 months or have other stable income
  • Have a debt to income ratio less than 45%
  • No minimum credit score requirement
  • Applicants with lower credit scores must have longer recent employment history and lower outstanding debt obligations

Revitalize Milwaukee

The Program

Rebuilding Together Greater Milwaukee preserves affordable housing and revitalizes vulnerable neighborhoods throughout the Milwaukee and Waukesha counties. The program provides FREE, professional home restoration, vital safety repairs and life-changing accessibility modifications for low-incone homeowners who are senior citizens, veterans and/or persons with disibilities.

Additional Eligibility/Participation Requirements

  • Homeowners must be 60 years of age or older and low-income OR a person living with a disability and low-income OR a veteran who is low-income
  • Homeowner of a single family home or jointly owned duplex home where both owners live and qualify in Milwaukee or Waukesha County (no condos, apartments, rental property, partly rented duplexes or mobile homes)
  • Homeowner's property must not be held in trust or a lide estate property
  • Homeowner has not recieved repaiors from RTGM in the past 2 years (this does not include emergency visits)
  • Homeowner must not own any other property
  • Homeowner cannot have a daycare or any other home-based business operating in the residence
  • Household income must fall within low-income guidelines based on 50% County Mean Income Limits (see website provided below)

The program provides zero percent interest loans of up to $15,000 for critical and minor home repairs. Lead Poisoning Prevention Program

City of Milwaukee Health Department

The Program

The program provides resources to abate lead based paint hazards in windows for property owners.

  • Note: If your home was built before 1978 it may contain lead paint and homes built before 1950 usually have the highest concentrations of lead paint. Repairing and remodeling painted surfaces in homes built before 1978, if done in an unsafe manner, can be hazardous to children. if lead dust is swallowed or breathed, it can cause lead poisoning, the #1 environmental health problem facing American children today.

    Lead poisoning, even at low levels in a child's body, can cause permanent brain damage and negatively affect children's behaviors and their ability to learn. Young children and pregnant women are at greatest risk. Protect your family by following safe remodeling procedures - whether you do the work yourself or hire a contractor to do it for you.

Additional Eligibility/Participation Requirements

  • Property must have been built before 1950
  • For owner occupied properties, children under age 3 must reside in the property
  • Owner is willing to pay up to 20% of costs plus $57 permit fee
  • Owner must be available for inspection with lead inspector
  • For rental properties, tenants must have income below 80% of Area Median Income (see website provided below) and property owner must agree to rent to income eligible families for at least three years after hazards are abated.

Area of Availability

This program provides resources for pre-1950 properties in zip codes: 53206, 53208, 53209, 53210, 53212, and 53216
